About me

Since early childhood, I was fascinated by drawing, starting from kindergarten and school, where I drew various scenes on desks. My talent was inherited from my mother, who was an artist and worked at the airport creating beautiful signs and portraits. My father, a civil aviation pilot, also worked there. After school, I entered university (1997-2002) and became deeply interested in painting. My teacher, Mr. Chilikin, at the Art Faculty, seeing my choice of pencil drawing for the diploma exam, shed tears and said: ‚ÄúYou are a wonderful painter, it is a pity you chose only drawing!‚Äù Unfortunately, he passed away later, but his words stayed with me. Since 2002, I focused on painting, winning first prizes for color composition. I created a series of oil paintings that amazed viewers at exhibitions. Each painting took months, and people often cried seeing my landscapes. I tried different businesses, but painting remained my passion for over 22 years. Traveling, especially to Vietnam, inspired me, and I returned to the canvas full of energy. My course reveals the real secrets of painting, exposing how education systems often suppress talent, showing that creativity comes from God, not the state. My course helps beginners and experienced artists alike to unleash their creative energy freely without unnecessary rules or techniques imposed by schools or state programs. It saves years of trial and error and filters out harmful information that could block your talent and creative flow.
